The goal of this website is to provide an accessible streamlined documentation and usage of brand guidelines for GHIBLI. Designers, front-end developers, and brand managers can use this style guide to apply brand consistency throughtout the development cyle of this product.

Branding

GHIBLI is a beauty product online store that focus on skincare products for all. This includes men and women that are looking for natural, non-animal tested skincare beauty products. Users age can range between 18-65, focusing on both men and women interested in skincare that works for their needs. GHIBLI partners with various well renowned skincare companies to provide their products within the store. Think of GHIBLI as the Sephora for all natural skincare products.

User Personas: Hypothetically, let's imagine a customer, named "Shiori" would like to purchase a skin cleanser for her night-time skin routine. Since she has sensitive skin, her main concern is that the cleanser that she's looking to buy might damager her skin. With the user goal in mind, GHIBLI provides additional product information that will help customer's like Shiori to purchase her skincare products with confidence.

    the colours

    The colour pallete used for GHIBLI consists of two natural tones colours. The primary colour will white and the secondary colour would be a light beige tone. These colours will be used throughout the entirety of the website branding.

    Primary colour. #FFFFFF
    Secondary colour. #FAF7F3
    All text. #000000
    Buttons. #EC9900

    the image

    The imagery used in GHIBLI incorporates actual people using the products. Studies have shown that humans gravitate towards products that they can imagine using. The imagery focuses on humans using the products in a real-world environment.
